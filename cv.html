Research and Teaching

Research

I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Engineering at Politecnico di Torino in October 2024. My thesis, titled "Securing IoT Gateways in Smart Home Environments" (PDF), focused on IoT security and smart home security.

My current research focuses primarily on automotive cybersecurity. I am involved in the COLTRANE-V project and working on Social Media Automotive Threat Intelligence (SOCMATI). I also conduct research on real-time embedded systems reliability, with a particular focus on FreeRTOS and fault injection techniques.

Teaching

Since March 2021, I have been one of the teaching assistants for "Applicazioni Web I" / "Web Application I" (M.Sc. in Computer Engineering). I prepared laboratory materials and solutions, supported students during lab sessions, occasionally gave lectures, and contributed to exam preparation and oral evaluations.

Since September 2024, I have also been one of the teachers of "Algorithms and Programming" (B.Sc. for Electronic and Communications Engineering), a course focused on advanced programming and design-oriented skills.

Earlier teaching support included courses such as Distributed Programming 1, Distributed Programming 2, Operating Systems, Computer Architectures, and Computer Science.
