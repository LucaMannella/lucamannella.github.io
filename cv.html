Research Activity

I am a postdoctoral research fellow at the Department of Control and Computer Engineering, part of the SMILIES research group. My research lies at the intersection of Cybersecurity, Internet of Things (IoT), Software Engineering, and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI).

My Ph.D. (completed in 2024) focused on supporting developers in the cybersecurity of IoT systems, especially smart home security. My Ph.D. thesis is titled "Securing IoT Gateways in Smart Home Environments".

Since March 2024, I have been working as a research assistant within the SMILIES research group. My recent work mainly focuses on automotive cybersecurity projects. I am involved in the COLTRANE-V research project and also working on a Social Media Automotive Threat Intelligence direction (SOCMATI).
